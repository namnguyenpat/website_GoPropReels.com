## The Dubai Firm Eyes America

Funding Pips has been one of the most talked-about growth stories in the prop trading world since its launch. Built out of Dubai with a sleek brand identity and aggressive trader-first positioning, the firm paid out over $110 million to traders before its first anniversary. Now it is making its most ambitious move yet: a structured entry into the United States retail trading market.

The US expansion, announced in early 2026, is not simply a matter of making the website available to American traders. It represents a deliberate infrastructure build: localized payment processing, USD-denominated accounts configured for US tax reporting, dedicated English-language customer support based on US time zones, and compliance consultation with financial services legal specialists familiar with the US regulatory environment.

## Why the US Market Is Both Attractive and Complex

For any international prop firm, the United States represents the single largest concentration of retail traders in the world. The spending power of American retail traders is well-documented, and the prop challenge model has found a receptive audience among the US day-trading community that grew substantially during the 2020-2022 retail trading boom.

But the US market comes with complications that do not exist in the UAE, EU, or APAC regions:

**Regulatory fragmentation.** Financial services regulation in the US operates across multiple federal and state jurisdictions. The CFTC, SEC, FinCEN, and individual state money transmission regulators each have potential claims on different aspects of a prop trading firm's operations.

**Payment processing friction.** US payment processors apply stricter know-your-customer and anti-money-laundering requirements than many international alternatives. Firms that rely on international payment rails sometimes face rejection rates or delays for US customers.

**Legal exposure.** The SEC's evolving stance on prop trading models — particularly around the question of whether funded accounts constitute securities — has created legal uncertainty that firms must navigate carefully.

Funding Pips has addressed these challenges through partnerships with US-based legal counsel and a payment infrastructure build that prioritizes domestic processing for American customers.

## What US Traders Get With Funding Pips

American traders accessing [Funding Pips](/forex/funding-pips-2b48e) through its US market offering will find a product lineup largely consistent with the firm's global offering, with some localized adjustments:

- Account funding and withdrawal via ACH, domestic wire, and major US-compatible digital payment options
- Evaluation accounts from $5,000 to $200,000 with the firm's standard two-phase challenge structure
- Profit targets of 8% Phase 1, 5% Phase 2 with a 5% daily and 10% overall drawdown limit
- Profit split starting at 80% and scaling toward 90% with performance milestones
- Customer support accessible during US Eastern and Pacific time zone business hours

## The Competitive Landscape in the US

The US prop trading market is not empty. FTMO, [FundedNext](/forex/fundednext-6gkdu), and numerous other international firms have served US traders for years without formal US market infrastructure. Domestic players including Topstep and Earn2Trade have built specifically for the American market.

Funding Pips' entry adds a well-capitalized, high-reputation international name to that domestic competitive set. For US traders, the result is more choice and more competitive pressure on pricing — which historically has meant better promotional offers and more trader-friendly terms.

## Industry Reaction

Other prop firms have watched Funding Pips' US market build with a combination of respect and concern. The firm's payout track record, combined with its willingness to invest in local infrastructure rather than simply accepting US traffic without support, signals serious long-term intent.

Several trading educators and community voices in the US prop trading space have begun featuring Funding Pips in their comparison content — a meaningful indicator of organic market penetration that precedes brand recognition among mainstream retail traders.

The US market entry is a significant moment not just for Funding Pips but for the international prop firm category broadly. When Dubai-based operators invest in US-specific compliance infrastructure, it signals that the global prop trading model has matured enough to sustain the complexity and cost of formal market expansion.

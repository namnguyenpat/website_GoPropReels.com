## The Scaling Pitch and the Reality Behind It

Every major prop firm offers some version of a "scaling plan": a mechanism through which successful traders can access progressively larger capital allocations as they demonstrate consistent profitability. The marketing language around scaling is often aspirational — "turn your $25K account into $2M" — but the mathematical reality of how scaling actually works, at what pace, and on what terms requires more careful examination.

This guide breaks down the real mathematics of scaling plans across the major prop firms so you can accurately calculate your realistic income trajectory.

## FTMO Scaling Plan: The Benchmark

[FTMO](/forex/ftmo-1vldc)'s scaling plan is one of the most established and clearly documented in the industry.

**Scaling triggers:** After each 10% growth milestone on the funded account, account size increases by 25%.

**Example progression from a $100,000 account:**

| Milestone | Account Size | Required Profit ($) |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|
| Start | $100,000 | — |
| +10% | $125,000 | $10,000 |
| +10% | $156,250 | $12,500 |
| +10% | $195,313 | $15,625 |
| +10% | $244,141 | $19,531 |
| +10% | $300,000 (cap) | ~$24,000 |

**Time mathematics at 5% monthly return:**
- Month 1: $100K × 5% = $5,000 × 80% split = $4,000 income
- After first scaling (month 2+): $125K × 5% = $6,250 × 80% split = $5,000 income
- At $300K with 90% split: $300K × 5% = $15,000 × 90% = $13,500/month

The mathematical reality: reaching $300K on FTMO's scaling plan at 5% monthly returns takes approximately 7-8 months. Monthly income at that scale with a 90% split is $13,500 — significant, achievable, but requiring sustained consistent performance over a prolonged period.

## FundedNext Scaling Plan

[FundedNext](/forex/fundednext-6gkdu)'s Stellar accounts offer scaling with slightly different triggers:

**Scaling trigger:** 10% profit growth on the funded account (consistent with FTMO)

**Account size increase:** 40% per milestone (more aggressive than FTMO's 25%)

**Starting profit split:** 90% (higher than FTMO's 80% starting point)

**Example from $100,000:**

| Milestone | Account Size | Monthly Income at 5% / 90% |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------------------|
| Start | $100,000 | $4,500 |
| +10% | $140,000 | $6,300 |
| +10% | $196,000 | $8,820 |
| +10% | $274,400 | $12,348 |

FundedNext's 40% account size increase per milestone means capital scales faster than FTMO per achieved milestone, and the 90% split from day one provides higher income at every stage. The trade-off: FundedNext's maximum account size is lower.

## Apex Trader Funding Scaling (Futures)

[Apex Trader Funding](/futures/apex-trader-funding-wvkmv) operates differently from forex prop firms. Rather than growing a single account, Apex's scaling model involves adding additional funded accounts:

**100% first withdrawal:** Your first withdrawal from each Apex account is 100% of profits.

**Multi-account model:** Apex allows up to 20 simultaneous funded accounts, each generating independent returns.

**Income multiplication:** 10 funded $50K Apex accounts at 3% monthly return each = $150,000 × 90% = $135,000/month theoretical maximum.

The practical reality: maintaining consistent positive returns across 10 simultaneous futures accounts requires extraordinary discipline, skill, and attention. Most traders run 2-5 accounts simultaneously at maximum.

## The Time Investment Honest Assessment

The scaling timelines assume consistent positive performance. The actual distribution of monthly returns includes losing months, breakeven months, and varying gain months. A more realistic modeled timeline:

**Conservative model (3 months at 5%, 1 month at 0%, 1 month at -2% out of 5 months):**
- Effective quarterly growth: ~13% instead of 15%
- Time to first FTMO scaling trigger: approximately 8-10 months instead of 6-7 months

**The honest takeaway:** Income from scaling plans is compounding over a 12-24 month horizon for most traders, not a 6-month sprint. Plan financially for the growth timeline, not the marketing timeline.

Scaling plans work. The mathematics are legitimate. The timeline is longer and the monthly return consistency required is higher than most traders initially expect. Understanding the real math is the first step to achieving the real result.

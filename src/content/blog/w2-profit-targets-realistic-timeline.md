## The Promise vs. The Reality

Prop firm marketing consistently implies rapid wealth: "Get funded in 30 days," "Trade your way to a $200,000 account," "Turn $350 into $10,000." These statements are technically possible under optimal conditions. The statistical reality of what actually happens for the average trader is considerably more nuanced.

This guide provides honest, data-grounded timelines for meeting profit targets across different account sizes, strategy types, and experience levels. Understanding what is realistically achievable is not pessimism — it is the foundation for sustainable planning.

## The Math of Standard Evaluation Targets

The typical two-phase evaluation requires:
- Phase 1: 8-10% profit target (usually 30 days)
- Phase 2: 5% profit target (usually 30-60 days)

Total time to funded account (if passing on first attempt): 60-90 days minimum.

The average daily return needed to hit an 8% target in 30 trading days (assuming 22 trading days in a month) is approximately 0.36% per day. This sounds trivial. In practice, it requires maintaining consistent performance without using drawdown limits as a crutch.

## Realistic Pass Timelines by Trader Level

### Beginner Traders (Under 1 Year Experience)

**First evaluation pass:** 3-6 months from starting evaluation attempts. Most beginners fail their first 2-4 evaluations before either improving enough to pass or abandoning the pursuit. The learning curve through failed evaluations is real and should be financially planned for.

**Realistic timeline to first funded account:** 4-8 months.

**Total likely evaluation fee spend before first pass:** $400-$1,500 depending on account size and number of attempts.

Beginners benefit most from starting with the smallest available account size (typically $5,000-$10,000) where evaluation fees are lowest, and treating early attempts as education rather than expected first-time success.

### Intermediate Traders (1-3 Years Experience)

**First evaluation pass:** 4-12 weeks is realistic for traders with established edge who are learning the prop-specific rules. The most common failure mode for intermediates is not strategy quality but rule compliance — consistency rules, news events, and overnight holding restrictions catch traders who were not reading carefully.

**Realistic timeline to first funded account:** 2-3 months.

### Experienced Traders (3+ Years with Consistent Track Record)

**First evaluation pass:** 3-6 weeks for traders with a proven edge who have studied the specific rules of their chosen firm. The evaluation is primarily an administrative hurdle rather than a skill test for this group.

**Realistic timeline to first funded account:** 6-12 weeks.

## Timeline to Meaningful Monthly Income

Passing a challenge is one milestone. Generating consistent, meaningful monthly income from a funded account is a separate and more demanding goal with a longer timeline:

### $500/month Target (Entry Level)

On a $50,000 account at 90% profit split:
- Required monthly profit: ~$556 (1.1% of account)
- Realistic for: Intermediate traders with a stable, tested strategy
- Timeline from first funded account activation: 1-3 months to establish consistency

### $2,000/month Target (Meaningful Supplemental Income)

Options:
- Single $200,000 account at 1.1% monthly profit with 90% split: $1,980
- Four $50,000 accounts at 1.1% monthly profit: ~$1,980

This is achievable for experienced traders and represents a realistic year-1 income target for someone seriously pursuing prop trading.

### $5,000+/month Target (Primary Income Potential)

- Requires either large single accounts ($200,000+) or a portfolio of multiple funded accounts
- Timeline from starting the prop trading journey: realistically 12-24 months for a trader who starts with legitimate experience and works seriously at the craft
- More commonly achieved by traders running 3-5 simultaneous funded accounts across multiple firms

## The Failure Rate Reality

The uncomfortable statistic: the large majority of evaluation account purchasers — estimates from various firm data points range from 70% to 90% — do not generate sustained monthly income from their funded accounts. Either they fail the evaluation, breach the funded account quickly, or achieve funded status but struggle to trade profitably under live conditions.

This does not mean the goal is unachievable. It means the timeline to sustainable income is longer than marketing suggests, the learning investment is real, and the traders who succeed treat this as a serious skill development process rather than a lottery ticket.

## What Makes a Prop Firm "Beginner-Friendly"?

Not all prop firms are built equal — especially for new traders. Here is what to look for when you are just getting started:

| Factor | Why It Matters for Beginners |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Low evaluation fee | Minimizes financial risk while you learn |
| Forgiving drawdown rules | More room for normal learning mistakes |
| No minimum trading days | Flexibility to take your time |
| Good customer support | Answers when you are confused |
| Educational resources | Helps you understand what you should be doing |
| Realistic profit targets | 8–10% is manageable; 20% in 30 days is a trap |

## Account Sizes: What Should You Start With?

This is one of the most common beginner questions.

**Start with $25,000 or $50,000 — not $100,000.**

Here is the logic:

- Lower fees mean less financial pressure
- Smaller targets are psychologically easier to hit
- You learn the firm's rules, platform, and your own discipline before scaling up
- If you fail, the reset cost is smaller

Many traders make the mistake of going straight to a $100,000 challenge. The larger the account, the higher the evaluation fee, and the more psychological weight sits on every trade.

## Mistakes Beginners Make When Choosing a Prop Firm

**Chasing the highest profit split.** A 90% split on a difficult challenge is worth less than an 80% split on a firm you can actually pass.

**Ignoring the drawdown type.** Static drawdown and trailing drawdown behave very differently. Trailing drawdown can catch beginners completely off-guard (see our dedicated guide on this topic).

**Not reading the fine print.** Some firms have consistency rules, inactivity clauses, or prohibited strategy lists buried deep in their terms. Read everything.

**Choosing based on influencer promotions.** Prop firms pay high affiliate commissions. Always research independently before buying.

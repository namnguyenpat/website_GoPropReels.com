## When the Market Goes Live — News Is the Director

Nothing moves a market like a central bank decision, a non-farm payrolls release, or an unexpected geopolitical headline. For funded traders, news events are simultaneously the biggest opportunity and the highest-stakes risk on the calendar.

The difference between a blown account and a highlight-reel trade often comes down to preparation, rule compliance, and execution speed.

---

## The Prop Firm News Problem

Here is the tension every funded trader faces: **news creates volatility, and volatility creates opportunity** — but prop firms built their risk models around controlling volatility. Many firms have strict rules about news trading that can result in immediate account termination.

Before you place a single trade around a news event, you need to know your firm's position on the following:

| Rule Type | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| No holding during news | Cannot have open positions within 2 minutes of high-impact events |
| No opening during news | Cannot open new positions within a news window |
| News blackout window | Defined period (e.g., 2 min before to 2 min after) where no trading is allowed |
| Prohibited instruments | Some firms ban trading XAUUSD or indices during news |
| No restriction at all | Firm allows full news trading — read carefully to confirm |

**Firms that typically restrict news trading:** FTMO, Audacity Capital, E8 Markets
**Firms that generally allow news trading:** FundedNext (with conditions), The Funded Trader, MyFundedFX

Always verify with the current terms — these policies change.

## High-Impact News Events That Move Markets

Not all economic data releases are equal. These are the events funded traders watch most closely:

### Tier 1 — Maximum Impact
- **Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)** — First Friday of the month, 8:30 AM ET. Moves USD pairs significantly.
- **Federal Reserve Interest Rate Decision** — 8 times per year. Can move 100+ pips in seconds.
- **CPI (Consumer Price Index)** — Monthly inflation data. Critical for rate expectations.
- **ECB, BOE, BOJ Rate Decisions** — Move EUR, GBP, and JPY pairs respectively.

### Tier 2 — High Impact
- **GDP data releases** — Quarterly. Major market mover.
- **Retail Sales** — Monthly consumer spending indicator.
- **FOMC Minutes** — Market re-evaluates Fed path.
- **Unemployment Claims** — Weekly indicator of labor market health.

### Tier 3 — Moderate Impact
- **PMI Data** — Manufacturing and services sector health.
- **Consumer Confidence** — Sentiment data.
- **Durable Goods Orders** — Forward-looking economic signal.

## News Trading Strategies That Work in Funded Accounts

### Strategy 1: The Pre-News Setup (For Firms That Allow It)

Enter a trade **before** the news event based on your directional bias from technical analysis. Set a wide stop-loss to absorb initial volatility. Let the trade run.

**Best for:** Traders with a strong technical read and firms that allow open positions during news.

**Risk:** Requires a firm that explicitly permits this. Verify first.

**Execution:**
1. Identify the technical trend and key levels 30 minutes before the event
2. Enter on a pullback to a key level with a 50–80 pip stop
3. Set target at the next major resistance/support
4. If news aligns with your bias, hold. If it contradicts, close immediately.

### Strategy 2: The Post-News Continuation Trade

This is the most commonly permitted approach in prop firms with news restrictions. **Wait for the initial spike to settle**, then enter in the direction of the sustained move.

**Best for:** All funded accounts. Most prop firms allow trading after the news window closes.

**Execution:**
1. Mark the news window (e.g., 2 minutes before and after the release)
2. Watch the initial reaction — identify which direction the market commits to
3. Wait for a pullback to a structural level (prior resistance/support, 50-period MA)
4. Enter on confirmation with a tight stop below the pullback low
5. Target the measured move from the news candle

### Strategy 3: The Range Breakout Play

Many currency pairs consolidate into tight ranges before major news. You can trade the breakout after the window clears.

**Execution:**
1. Mark the high and low of the 30-minute pre-news range
2. After the news window closes, place entry orders above the high and below the low
3. The order that fills is your trade. Cancel the other immediately.
4. Stop-loss goes just inside the range.

## The News Trading Calendar Setup

Every professional news trader uses **Forex Factory** (forexfactory.com) as their primary economic calendar. The red folder icons indicate high-impact events.

**Your pre-session routine:**
- Check Forex Factory for the current day's high-impact releases
- Mark the times and instruments affected
- Decide in advance: am I avoiding this window entirely, or am I prepared to trade it?
- Set alerts on your phone for 15 minutes and 5 minutes before each release

## Risk Management for News Trading

News events can trigger slippage — your order fills at a worse price than your stop-loss level. This is not a broker error; it is the market's response to a sudden liquidity gap.

**Protect yourself:**
- Use smaller position sizes on news trades (50% of your normal size)
- Widen your stop-loss to account for the initial spike
- Never expect a precise fill during the first 60 seconds of a high-impact release
- Have a maximum loss per news event — stop trading for the day if it hits

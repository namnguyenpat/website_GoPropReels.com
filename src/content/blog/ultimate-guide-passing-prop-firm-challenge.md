## What Is a Prop Firm Challenge?

A prop firm challenge is a simulated evaluation period where traders must hit a profit target while respecting strict risk rules. Pass, and the firm funds your live account. The typical structure looks like this:

| Phase | Goal | Common Rules |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Phase 1 | Hit 8–10% profit target | Max 5% daily loss, 10% total drawdown |
| Phase 2 | Hit 4–5% profit target | Same risk limits |
| Funded | Trade and earn profit share | Ongoing risk compliance |

Some firms offer **one-step** evaluations (single phase, higher target), while **two-step** models split the goal across two phases. The two-step format is generally more forgiving and widely preferred by professionals.

## Know the Rules Before You Trade a Single Lot

This is non-negotiable. Most traders fail challenges not from bad strategy — but from rule violations they did not see coming.

**Study these rules for your specific firm:**

- **Daily drawdown limit** — is it 5% of initial balance or 5% of the prior day's closing equity?
- **Maximum drawdown** — static (fixed from starting balance) or trailing (moves with your peak equity)?
- **Minimum trading days** — some firms require 5–10 active trading days regardless of how fast you hit the target
- **Consistency rule** — some firms cap your best day's profit as a percentage of total profits
- **News trading restrictions** — many firms prohibit holding positions within 2 minutes of high-impact news events
- **Weekend holding policy** — can you hold trades over Saturday and Sunday?

Read the firm's FAQ. Then read it again.

## The Right Strategy for Evaluation Accounts

Not every strategy that works in live trading works well in evaluations. The challenge environment rewards **consistency over aggression**.

### What Works Well

- **Trend-following with clear structure** — enter on pullbacks, ride the move, cut losses fast
- **Asian session breakouts** — lower volatility, predictable ranges
- **London open setups** — high liquidity, clean price action
- **Session scalping** — small, high-probability entries with disciplined exits

### What Gets Traders Killed

- **Revenge trading after a losing day** — this is the fastest path to violating the daily drawdown
- **Overleveraging** — the temptation to "get it all back in one trade" is the enemy
- **Holding through high-impact news** — not worth the whipsaw risk in an evaluation

## Risk Management: The Director's Formula

Think of risk management as your production budget. Overspend in one scene, and the whole film collapses.

**The 1% Rule:**
Risk no more than 1% of your account balance per trade. On a $100,000 challenge, that is $1,000 maximum risk per position.

**The Daily Cap:**
Never risk more than 2–3% of account balance in a single day. If two trades hit their stop-losses, you walk away from the screen. Full stop.

**The "Reset Day" Protocol:**
If you lose more than 1.5% in any session, close the platform, review what happened, and return tomorrow. The challenge has time limits — but most give you 30–60 days. Use them.

## Building Your Challenge Timeline

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Week 1 | Low risk, 0.5% per trade. Find your rhythm. |
| Week 2 | Scale to 1% if performing well. Hit minimum trading days. |
| Week 3 | Press toward 60–70% of profit target. No rushing. |
| Week 4 | Protect what you have. Small positions, clean exits. |

The goal is to reach the profit target with **at least 4–5% of drawdown buffer remaining**. You do not want to crawl across the finish line.

## Mindset: The Most Underrated Prop Firm Edge

The top 5% of challenge passers share one trait — they treat the evaluation like it is real capital, not play money. The moment you think "it's just a fee I can repay" is the moment your discipline evaporates.

**Practice these mental frameworks:**

- **Process over outcome** — judge each day on quality of execution, not P&L
- **Identity-based discipline** — "I am a professional trader, professionals follow rules"
- **Pre-session preparation** — know your levels, know your bias, know your maximum risk before the market opens

## The Final Scene: Submission Day

When you are close to hitting the target, the temptation is to push harder. Resist it. Reduce your position sizes to half. Protect your cushion. Some of the most spectacular challenge failures happen in the final 1% of the journey.

Cross the finish line with margin to spare. Your funded account is not the end of the story — it is the opening act.
